# Meridian Professional Services — Client Onboarding Handbook

**Version:** 3.2  
**Effective Date:** January 2026  
**Classification:** Internal Use Only

---

## 1. Purpose

This handbook defines the standard policies, procedures, and requirements for onboarding new clients at Meridian Professional Services. All team members involved in client engagement must follow these guidelines to ensure compliance, quality, and consistency across our Legal, Construction Advisory, and Management Consulting practices.

---

## 2. Onboarding Process Overview

Every new client engagement follows a five-stage onboarding lifecycle:

| Stage | Description | Responsible Party | Target Duration |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **1. Intake** | Initial client request received and logged | Engagement Coordinator | 1 business day |
| **2. Due Diligence** | Background check, conflict of interest screening | Compliance Team | 3 business days |
| **3. Scope & Proposal** | Engagement scope defined, proposal or engagement letter drafted | Engagement Lead | 5 business days |
| **4. Approval** | Internal review and sign-off by Practice Director | Practice Director | 2 business days |
| **5. Activation** | Client record created, team assigned, project initiated | Operations Team | 1 business day |

**Total target duration:** 12 business days from intake to activation.

---

## 3. Intake Requirements

### 3.1 Required Information

The following information must be collected during intake:

- **Client name** (legal entity or individual)
- **Primary contact name and email**
- **Engagement type** (Legal Advisory, Litigation Support, Construction Management, Building Inspection, Strategy Consulting, Technology Advisory, etc.)
- **Brief description of scope** (2–3 sentences minimum)
- **Estimated engagement value** (if known)
- **Urgency level** (Standard, Priority, Urgent)
- **Referral source** (if applicable)

### 3.2 Document Collection

Clients must provide the following documents before proceeding to Due Diligence:

- Signed Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)
- Government-issued identification for primary contact
- Certificate of incorporation (for corporate clients)
- Any relevant project documentation (contracts, permits, proposals, etc.)

---

## 4. Due Diligence & Compliance

### 4.1 Conflict of Interest Check

All new engagements require a conflict of interest screening. The Compliance Team must verify:

- No existing engagements with adverse parties
- No personal relationships that could compromise objectivity
- No regulatory restrictions on the engagement type

**If a conflict is identified:** The engagement must be escalated to the Ethics Committee before proceeding. The Ethics Committee has 5 business days to render a decision.

### 4.2 Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Screening

For engagements exceeding $50,000 in estimated value, the following additional checks are required:

- Sanctions list screening (OFAC, EU, UN)
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEP) check
- Source of funds verification

### 4.3 Industry-Specific Requirements

| Practice Area | Additional Requirement |
|---------------|----------------------|
| **Legal** | Bar association standing verification for referred counsel |
| **Construction** | Valid contractor license verification, insurance certificate review |
| **Consulting** | Non-compete clause review for strategic engagements |

## 5. Engagement Scope & Pricing

### 5.1 Scope Definition

Every engagement must have a clearly defined scope document that includes:

- Objectives and deliverables
- Timeline and milestones
- Team composition and roles
- Out-of-scope items (explicitly stated)
- Assumptions and dependencies

### 5.2 Pricing Guidelines

| Engagement Type | Standard Rate Range | Billing Model |
|-----------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Legal Advisory | $250–$450/hour | Hourly |
| Litigation Support | $300–$500/hour | Hourly or Fixed Fee |
| Construction Management | 3–8% of project value | Percentage |
| Building Inspection | $2,000–$15,000 | Fixed Fee |
| Strategy Consulting | $350–$600/hour | Hourly or Retainer |
| Technology Advisory | $275–$500/hour | Hourly or Fixed Fee |

All pricing must be approved by the Practice Director before inclusion in a proposal.

## 6. Approval Workflow

### 6.1 Approval Authority

| Engagement Value | Approver |
|-----------------|----------|
| Under $25,000 | Engagement Lead |
| $25,000–$100,000 | Practice Director |
| $100,000–$500,000 | Managing Partner |
| Over $500,000 | Executive Committee |

### 6.2 Approval Criteria

The approver must verify:

- [ ] Due diligence completed with no unresolved issues
- [ ] Engagement scope is clearly defined
- [ ] Pricing aligns with guidelines
- [ ] Required resources are available
- [ ] Client has provided all required documents

## 7. Client Communication Standards

### 7.1 Response Time Requirements

| Communication Type | Maximum Response Time |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| Initial inquiry | 4 business hours |
| Status update request | 1 business day |
| Urgent matter | 2 hours |
| Formal correspondence | 2 business days |

### 7.2 Communication Tone

All client communications must be:

- **Professional:** Use formal business language
- **Clear:** Avoid jargon unless the client is familiar with the terms
- **Proactive:** Anticipate questions and address them before they arise
- **Documented:** All substantive communications must be logged

## 8. Data Protection & Confidentiality

### 8.1 Information Classification

| Classification | Description | Examples |
|---------------|-------------|----------|
| **Public** | May be shared externally | Published case studies, firm brochure |
| **Internal** | Firm employees only | This handbook, internal procedures |
| **Confidential** | Engagement team only | Client documents, engagement details |
| **Restricted** | Named individuals only | M&A details, litigation strategy |

### 8.2 Data Handling Rules

- Client data must be stored in approved systems only (firm SharePoint, approved cloud storage)
- No client data may be sent to personal email accounts
- Physical documents must be stored in locked cabinets
- All client data must be disposed of per the firm's retention schedule after engagement completion

## 10. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can we onboard a client without a signed NDA?**  
A: No. The NDA must be signed before any substantive work or information sharing begins. Preliminary discussions are permitted.

**Q: What if the client needs to start urgently but due diligence isn't complete?**  
A: An Urgent Engagement Waiver can be requested from the Practice Director. Limited preliminary work may begin while due diligence continues, but no confidential client information may be shared with the engagement team until screening is complete.

**Q: How do we handle a client that operates in multiple practice areas?**  
A: Create separate onboarding records for each practice area. A single client may have multiple active engagements across Legal, Construction, and Consulting.

**Q: What is the maximum number of active engagements per Engagement Lead?**  
A: The recommended maximum is 8 active engagements. Exceptions require Practice Director approval.
