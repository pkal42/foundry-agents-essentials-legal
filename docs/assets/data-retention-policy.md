# Meridian Legal — Data Retention Policy

**Policy Number:** ML-POL-005  
**Version:** 1.4  
**Effective Date:** January 2026  
**Classification:** Internal Use Only  
**Approved By:** Managing Partner & Chief Information Officer

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the standards for retaining, archiving, and disposing of client files, firm records, and electronic data at Meridian Legal. All personnel must follow this policy when handling any firm or client data.

---

## 2. Retention Schedule

### 2.1 Client Matter Files

| Document Type | Retention Period | Trigger Event |
|---|---|---|
| Active matter files | Duration of engagement + 7 years | Matter closure date |
| Closed matter files (litigation) | 10 years after final disposition | Final judgment or settlement |
| Closed matter files (corporate/transactions) | 7 years after completion | Transaction closing date |
| Closed matter files (regulatory compliance) | 10 years after completion | Final regulatory approval |
| Closed matter files (employment law) | 7 years after completion | Resolution date |
| Closed matter files (intellectual property) | Life of the IP right + 7 years | Expiration or abandonment |

### 2.2 Financial Records

| Document Type | Retention Period |
|---|---|
| Client invoices and billing records | 7 years |
| Trust account records (IOLTA) | 10 years |
| Engagement letters and fee agreements | Duration of engagement + 7 years |

### 2.3 Compliance Records

| Document Type | Retention Period |
|---|---|
| Conflict screening records | Related matter retention + 7 years |
| Ethics committee determinations | Permanent |
| Ethical wall documentation | Related matter retention + 7 years |

---

## 3. Legal Holds

A legal hold suspends all normal retention and destruction schedules for documents related to pending or anticipated litigation, government investigations, or audits.

**Procedures:**

1. The **Managing Partner** or **General Counsel** issues a written legal hold notice
2. All personnel with relevant documents are notified in writing
3. Automated deletion policies are suspended for documents within scope
4. The hold remains in effect until formally released in writing

Failure to comply with a legal hold may result in court sanctions, adverse inference instructions, and disciplinary action.

---

## 4. Destruction Procedures

When a document reaches the end of its retention period and is not subject to a legal hold:

1. The **Records Management Team** identifies eligible documents quarterly
2. A destruction list is reviewed and approved by the **Practice Director**
3. Physical documents are destroyed via **cross-cut shredding** by a certified vendor
4. Electronic documents are permanently deleted using **secure deletion** methods
5. A **Certificate of Destruction** is generated and retained permanently

If a client requests return of original documents, copies are retained for the remainder of the retention period and a return receipt is obtained.
