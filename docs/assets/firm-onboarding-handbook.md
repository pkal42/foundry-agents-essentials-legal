# Meridian Legal — Client Onboarding Handbook

**Version:** 3.2  
**Effective Date:** January 2026  
**Classification:** Internal Use Only

---

## 1. Purpose

This handbook defines the standard policies and procedures for onboarding new clients at Meridian Legal. All team members must follow these guidelines across our Corporate & Transactions, Litigation, Regulatory & Compliance, Employment Law, and Intellectual Property practice areas.

---

## 2. Onboarding Process Overview

Every new client engagement follows a five-stage onboarding lifecycle:

| Stage | Description | Responsible Party | Target Duration |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **1. Intake** | Initial client request received and logged | Engagement Coordinator | 1 business day |
| **2. Due Diligence** | Background check, conflict of interest screening | Compliance Team | 3 business days |
| **3. Scope & Proposal** | Engagement scope defined, proposal drafted | Engagement Lead | 5 business days |
| **4. Approval** | Internal review and sign-off | Practice Director | 2 business days |
| **5. Activation** | Client record created, team assigned | Operations Team | 1 business day |

**Total target duration:** 12 business days from intake to activation.

---

## 3. Intake Requirements

The following information must be collected during intake:

- **Client name** (legal entity or individual)
- **Primary contact name and email**
- **Engagement type** (Legal Advisory, Litigation, Corporate & Transactions, Regulatory Compliance, Employment Law, Intellectual Property, etc.)
- **Brief description of scope** (2–3 sentences minimum)
- **Estimated engagement value** (if known)
- **Urgency level** (Standard, Priority, Urgent)

Clients must provide a signed NDA, government-issued identification, certificate of incorporation (corporate clients), and any relevant project documentation before proceeding to Due Diligence.

---

## 4. Due Diligence & Compliance

All new engagements require a conflict of interest screening — see the Conflict of Interest Policy (ML-POL-003) for details. **If a conflict is identified:** the engagement must be escalated to the Ethics Committee before proceeding. The Ethics Committee has 5 business days to render a decision.

For engagements exceeding $50,000, additional AML screening is required: sanctions list screening (OFAC, EU, UN), Politically Exposed Persons (PEP) check, and source of funds verification.

| Practice Area | Additional Requirement |
|---------------|----------------------|
| **Litigation** | Statute of limitations verification, jurisdictional analysis |
| **Corporate & Transactions** | Regulatory filing deadline verification, board resolution review |
| **Employment Law** | EEOC/state agency filing status check |

## 6. Approval Workflow

| Engagement Value | Approver |
|-----------------|----------|
| Under $25,000 | Engagement Lead |
| $25,000–$100,000 | Practice Director |
| $100,000–$500,000 | Managing Partner |
| Over $500,000 | Executive Committee |

The approver must verify: due diligence completed, engagement scope defined, pricing aligned with guidelines, resources available, and all required client documents received.

## 8. FAQ

**Q: Can we onboard a client without a signed NDA?**  
A: No. The NDA must be signed before any substantive work begins. Preliminary discussions are permitted.

**Q: What if the client needs to start urgently but due diligence isn't complete?**  
A: An Urgent Engagement Waiver can be requested from the Practice Director. Limited preliminary work may begin while due diligence continues.
